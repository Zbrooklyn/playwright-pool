# User Experience Analysis — Blog Editor Page

## First Impressions

**What do I think this page is for?**
It looks like some kind of writing tool — like a place where you write and edit a blog post or article. The big title at the top says something about "ecommerce agency vs freelancer vs in-house" so someone is writing a business article here.

**What stands out first?**
On desktop, the title of the article jumps out first because it's the biggest, boldest text. That feels right — I'd want to know what I'm editing. The toolbar at the top with all the little icons is also noticeable, though I'm not sure what all of them do.

On mobile, the big blue "Update" button in the top right catches my eye immediately. That's good — if I'm here to save my work, I know where to go.

**Does the page feel trustworthy and professional?**
It looks clean and simple, which is good. It feels like a real tool, not something sketchy. The text is well-written and readable. It reminds me a bit of Google Docs or WordPress — familiar enough that I wouldn't be worried about using it.

## Confusion Points

**Is anything confusing?**
- On desktop, there's a row of tiny icons in the toolbar (the formatting bar). I can recognize bold, italic, and underline, but there are a bunch of other little icons that I'd have to hover over to figure out. Some look like tiny squares or grids — no idea what those do without clicking them.
- The tabs at the very top say "Rich Text," "Markdown," "HTML," "Edit," "Preview," "Split." I know what Edit and Preview probably mean, but "Markdown" and "HTML" sound like computer stuff. I wouldn't know which one to pick. "Rich Text" is already selected and that seems to be working fine, so I'd probably just leave it alone.
- On mobile, the title gets cut off — it says "Ecommerce agency vs freelancer vs in-ho..." and just stops. I can't read the full title, which is a little annoying.

**Are there labels or instructions I don't understand?**
- "Heading 2" with a little dropdown arrow on the left side — I sort of get that it means the text size/style, but it's small and easy to miss.
- At the bottom of the desktop view, there's a bar that says something like "85.7%" and "1716 words" and "11,852 chars" and "SEO 63%." The word count is helpful, but "SEO 63%" doesn't mean much to me. What's SEO? Is 63% good or bad?
- On mobile, the bottom bar shows "02:30 PM" and some icons, plus "7m," "1716 words," "11,052 chars," "SEO 63%." Same confusion about SEO.

**Can you tell what's clickable and what isn't?**
Mostly yes. The toolbar buttons look like buttons. The "Update" button is clearly a button because it's blue. But on desktop, the top navigation tabs (Rich Text, Markdown, HTML, Edit, Preview, Split) are just plain text — they don't look super clickable. I might not realize they're tabs at first glance.

## Frustration Points

**Is any text too small to read comfortably?**
- On desktop, the toolbar icons are pretty small. If I didn't already know what bold/italic/underline looked like, I'd struggle.
- The bottom status bar text on both views is quite small — the word count, character count, and SEO percentage are tiny.
- On desktop, everything in the actual article area is a comfortable reading size.

**Is anything cut off or incomplete?**
- On mobile, the article title is definitely cut off. It just ends with "in-ho..." That's frustrating because the title is important.
- On desktop, the bottom of the page seems to cut off mid-sentence in what looks like a comparison table or chart area. I can see "Factor Agency Freelancer In-house" and "Monthly cost range $2,000 - $15,000+ $500 - $5,000 (project-" and it just stops. If I wanted to see the full table, I'd need to scroll.

**Does anything look broken or unfinished?**
Nothing looks broken exactly. The page looks functional. On mobile, the formatting toolbar feels a bit cramped — the icons are spread out but there are only a few visible (Bold, Italic, Underline, Strikethrough, and a couple of text formatting ones). There might be more options I'm missing.

**Would you feel lost navigating this page?**
Not really. It's a writing tool — I'd type in the big white area and hit "Update" when I'm done. The basics are clear enough. But if I needed to do something more advanced (like switch to a different editing mode or use one of those mystery toolbar icons), I might get stuck.

## Task Completion

**If I wanted to write or edit this article, could I figure out how?**
Yes, absolutely. The main editing area is obvious — it's the big white space with text in it. I'd click in there and start typing. The bold/italic buttons are familiar. I'd hit "Update" to save.

**Are the steps obvious?**
For basic editing, yes. Write your text, format it with the toolbar, hit Update. Simple enough.

For anything beyond that — like switching between Rich Text and Markdown modes, or understanding what the "Split" view does — I'd probably need to experiment or look for a help guide.

**Is important information easy to find?**
The word count at the bottom is handy. The SEO score seems useful if I knew what it meant. The "Close" button in the top left (desktop) is easy to spot if I want to leave.

## Overall Feeling

**Confidence score: 7 out of 10**
I could definitely use this page for basic writing and editing. It feels familiar enough — like a simpler version of Word or Google Docs. I'd lose confidence if I needed to do anything fancy, because some of the icons and options are unclear. But for just writing an article and hitting save? No problem.

**Would I come back?**
Yes, I'd come back. It's clean, it doesn't feel overwhelming, and it works for writing. I wouldn't go looking for an alternative unless I ran into a specific problem.

**One thing I'd change to make it easier to use:**
I'd make that title on mobile show the full text instead of cutting it off. The title is the most important thing — I need to see what article I'm editing. Either make the text wrap to a second line or let me tap it to see the whole thing. On desktop, I'd also want little labels or tooltips on the toolbar icons so I don't have to guess what each one does.
